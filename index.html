I hope to work with kids who are struggling with mental illness and working to focus on students who don't feel heard. Psychology is something I've always been interested in even as a kid, understanding feelings and thoughts and why we do what we do is something that has always fascinated me. I believe kids are the ones who need the most help right now, especially coming back from the pandemic. Unfortunately, children are the most neglected in terms of psychological help nowadays and I want to normalize therapy and mental health help in our society.

I grew up in Fremont, California, where it was common among teenagers to feel obligated to keep to themselves. Speaking with many peers in my grade and from my city, we often felt very alone even in cases where we had friends. Counselors were rarely present on campus to help us through academic help, let alone, psychological help. I think it's important for kids attending schools with this kind of conditions to have someone to listen to their problems and provide professional advice. I'm willing to accept whatever pay as my main goal as a psychologists is to provide honest professional opinions to help students struggling with their mental health.

In today's society, mental health has become a more serious subject and there are many new patients looking for the right therapist for them. I believe I can be a great asset to an agency looking for someone to listen and take patients seriously. Following this recent pandemic, jobs are becoming harder to handle and what better way than hiring new psychologists to help day to day workers get through this tough time.
